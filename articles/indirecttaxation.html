Indirect Taxation

Indirect Taxation: It is the tax whose effect is directly on the consumer. The person who consume the goods & services shall bear all the tax burden. Indirect Tax Include: Excise Duty, Value Added Tax, Custom Duty, Service Tax, Central Sales Tax etc. Some of the Indirect taxes are overcome by only one Tax called GST (Goods & Service Tax) the tax which are overcome are: Excise Duty, Service Tax, Central Sales Tax, Value Added Tax.

Example of Indirect Tax : A retailer buy a good from the manufacturer and pay tax on it called Value Added Tax (VAT) & after that the retailer sell it to the consumer & Consumer pay the tax  on the price which is bear by retailer plus the profit of the retailer. This may cause cascading effect as consumer will pay tax on tax.

Regressive Nature of Indirect Taxes:

Indirect taxes are commonly used & imposed by the government to generate revenue. Indirect tax is equally bear by the tax payer, no matter the person bear the tax is rich or poor. Indirect tax is regressive as it would be a heavy burden of the lower income people.

Service Tax:

It is a tax which is levied on the services provided by an entity . If an entity provided any services then that service provider is liable to pay service tax & that service tax is bear by the recipient & deposited to the central govt.

Excise Duty:

Excise duty is implemented on those goods which are manufactured in India . The liability of Central Excise duty arises when the goods manufactured. This tax is paid by the manufacturer, who passes its effect to the customers & recover the same from them.

Value Added Tax(VAT):

This tax is imposed on the sale of movable goods. It is a multi-point destination based tax, with tax being levied on value addition at each stage of transaction in the production/distribution chain.

VAT is a tax which is implied on the final consumption of goods /services & is ultimately borne by the consumer.

Custom Duty:

This tax is levied on goods which are imported into India. In some cases, it is also levied when the goods are exported from India.

Custom Act, 1962, provides for levy & collection of duty on imports & exports, Exports/Imports procedure, prohibitions on importations & exportation of goods, etc.

Securities Transaction Tax (STT):

It is a type of tax levied on the sale/purchase of securities through Indian Stock Exchange. The Securities include Shares, Mutual Funds, F & O Transactions etc. This tax is implemented w.e.f 1st April,2004.

To introduce STT the reason behind this is to lower the tax on short term capital gain & to make the long term capital gain exempted from the levy of any tax.

Stamp Duty: 

It is levied by the state govt. on transfer of any immovable property in their state. It is also levied by the govt on all the legal documents. & it may vary from state to state.

Entertainment Tax:

It is levied on every financial transaction which is related to entertainment & is reserved primarily for the state govt. Some example of Entertainment tax are: Amusement Park, PVR, Video Games etc

There are several other indirect tax also which are sales tax, octroi tax, luxury tax, etc.
